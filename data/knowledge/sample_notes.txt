Introduction to DNA Sequencing

DNA sequencing is the process of determining the precise order of nucleotides 
within a DNA molecule. Modern sequencing technologies, particularly Next 
Generation Sequencing (NGS), can sequence billions of base pairs in a single run.

FASTQ File Format

FASTQ files store both the nucleotide sequence and the corresponding quality 
scores for each base. Each record consists of four lines: the sequence identifier 
starting with @, the raw sequence, a separator line starting with +, and the 
quality scores encoded in ASCII format.

Quality scores use Phred encoding. A Phred score of 30 means there is a 1 in 
1000 chance of an incorrect base call. A score of 20 means 1 in 100 chance. 
Most analyses filter out bases with Phred scores below 20.

GC Content Analysis

GC content refers to the percentage of guanine and cytosine bases in a DNA 
sequence. Different organisms have characteristic GC content ranges. Human 
genome GC content is approximately 41 percent. High GC content can indicate 
coding regions or CpG islands, which are often associated with gene promoters.

RNA Sequencing

RNA-seq measures gene expression by sequencing messenger RNA molecules. 
Reads are aligned to a reference genome, counted per gene, and then compared 
between conditions using statistical methods such as DESeq2 or edgeR to 
identify differentially expressed genes.
