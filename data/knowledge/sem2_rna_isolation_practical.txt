RNA- similar to DNA but with several key differences

Made up of a repeating strand of nucleotides, contains all 3 components similar to DNA (phosphate, sugar and nitrogen base) 

Sugar in RNA is RIBOSE

Contain nitrogen base Uracil (U) instead of Thymine (T). Uracil binds to Adenine 

RNA is single stranded

Messenger RNA (mRNA)
Transfer RNA (tRNA)
Ribosomal RNA (rRNA)

Hypoxia- Insufficient oxygen
Replicate ischaemic stroke
Using two enzymatic reactions


Glucose Oxidase  (GOx) reaction:
Glucose Oxidation: Glucose oxidase oxidises glucose in the presence of molecular oxygen, producing gluconic acid and hydrogen peroxide.

Oxygen Consumption: The reaction consumes oxygen, and as a result, local oxygen levels decrease in the vicinity of the enzyme's action.


Catalase reaction: 
Catalase is an enzyme that breaks down hydrogen peroxide (H₂O₂) into water (H₂O) and oxygen (O₂)

Catalase offers limited oxygen recovery- still net decrease

Gox/Cat-induced Hypoxia

Induces a hypoxic state within minutes (you decide the length of time)
