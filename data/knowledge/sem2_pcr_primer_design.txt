PCR and primer design

Outline of PCR steps

Dissociation of double strand (ds) DNA
	- 95degC
Annealing of short oligonucleotide ‘primer’ to single-strand (ss) DNA
	- ~55-65 degC
Extension of dsDNA using a polymerase enzyme
	- 72degC
	- Thermo-stable polymerase from Thermus aquaticus (Taq)
Repeat
	- doubling of DNA of gene of interest in each ‘cycle’
	- 1 billion copies after 30 cycles

PCR and primer design

Primer design

Two “primers” which are DNA fragments with complementarity to a desired DNA sequence
	- one in forward orientation
	- one in reverse orientation

Size of ‘amplicon’ is determined by the distance between two primers

Binding parameters of primer to DNA primarily determined by number of hydrogen bonds (A:T = 2; C:G = 3), primer length, and how many times the sequence appears in the genome

Primer design

How specific are primers?

1 base pair		=	4^1 (4) possibilities
2 base pairs	=	4^2 (16) possibilities
3 base pairs	=	4^3 (64) possibilities
…
20 base pairs	=	4^20 (1 trillion possibilities)

Human genome	=	3.2 billion nucleotides (haploid)
By chance, a 20 bp sequence should only exist < once in the genome
