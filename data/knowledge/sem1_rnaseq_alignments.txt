11 SEM1 24/25 BIS7019-B – ‘Omics’ Techniques in Biology and Healthcare
Alignment-free (pseudo-alignment) quantification:
Goals:
1. Identify which transcript each read originated from
2. Count number of reads associated with each transcript
Process:
1. Quantify transcript abundances upon
identifying potential transcripts from which
read originated from (rather than upon exact
position of reads in the transcripts)
2. Infer gene counts from transcript abundances
What’s needed?
• Reads
• Indexed reference transcriptome
• Pseudo-aligner tool
Alignment and quantification

13 SEM1 24/25 BIS7019-B – ‘Omics’ Techniques in Biology and Healthcare
Genome alignment vs. pseudo-alignment conceptualised:
Pseudo-alignment:
• Deduce transcripts that
could have generated a
particular read
Example sequence = DNAHX
Cross reference its components with
transcripts in transcriptome
Transcripts without a ‘D’ cannot
possibly be matches = ignore them

14 SEM1 24/25 BIS7019-B – ‘Omics’ Techniques in Biology and Healthcare
Genome alignment vs. pseudo-alignment conceptualised:
Benefits of pseudo-alignment:
• ↑ computational performance (↓ time + ↓ power) without losing accuracy
• Transcript-level estimates can improve gene-level inferences
• Outputs ‘ready made’ for input into isoform analyses tools
Alignment and quantification
